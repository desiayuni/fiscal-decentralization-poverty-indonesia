\# VSLA Replication Notes



\## Paper

Impact of Village Savings and Loans Associations: Evidence from a Cluster Randomized Trial



\## Journal

Journal of Development Economics (2016)



\## Research Objective

This paper examines whether participation in Village Savings and Loan Associations (VSLAs) improves financial access and household welfare outcomes in rural Malawi.



\## Empirical Strategy

\- Cluster Randomized Controlled Trial (RCT)

\- Intention-to-Treat (ITT) estimation

\- OLS regression with clustered standard errors

\- Block fixed effects included



\## Data

\- Household-level survey data

\- Malawi

\- Baseline and post-treatment observations



\## Key Workflow Completed

\- Setup reproducible folder structure

\- Loaded and inspected dataset in STATA

\- Generated log-transformed variables

\- Conducted baseline balance regression

\- Estimated treatment effect on loan access



\## Preliminary Finding

The treatment effect on loan access (`anyloan`) was positive and statistically significant.



Coefficient:

0.122



P-value:

0.002



Interpretation:

Participation in the VSLA treatment increased the probability of accessing loans.
